Joynagar'er Moa

Jaynagarer Moa is a seasonal Bengali sweetmeat delicacy prepared from date palm jaggery and Kanakchur khoi. This variety of Moa originated in Jaynagar of the South 24 Parganas district in the Indian state of West Bengal.

Ingredients

  • Kanakchur Khoi or Aromatic paddy – 250 gm
  • Khoya Kheer – 100 gm
  • Khoya kheer – Dry for dusting
  • Ghee – as per need
  • Raisins – one per piece
  • Cardamom Powder- 1 tsp
  • Sugar – 2 Cup (or depending on sweetness needed)

Steps

  1. Add 2 cups of sugar and 2 cups of water in a pan.
  2. Boil it on a high flame for about 5 mins.
  3. When the sugar dissolves in water, keep stirring and boiling it on low flame.
  4. After 6 to 8 minutes the [i]chasni[/i] will be ready. Hold this thick mixture between your two fingers. If a single string is formed as you separate your fingers, the sugar syrup is ready.
  5. Let it cool for 5 minutes.
  6. In the authentic Joynagrer Moa recipe, the jaggery [i](nolen gur)[/i] is mixed in equal quantity with sugar to form this string.
  7. Now add [i]Kanakchur Khoi[/i] slowly in the sugar syrup and keep stirring.
  8. After mixing thoroughly, cover it very well with a cloth or a lid and keep it for minimum 10 hours or overnight.
  9. Next morning, remove the lid and add grated Khoya kheer. Mix it very well.
  10. Now add 1/2 tsp of the cardamom powder and 1 teaspoon ghee. Mix it very well.
  11. Make small balls by this mixture to shape it like Joynagarer Moa.
  12. Now spread the dry khoya kheer on the top of all Joynagarer Moas.
  13. Garnish the Joynagarer Moa with a Raisin each.
  14. Seasonal Bengali Sweet Recipe Joynagarer Moa is ready to serve
  15. It may not be the authentic one if you dont have Jolen Gur, but it is closer with Sugar and lot of sweetness while making.
